Information Retrieval with Verbose Queries

Full Day Tutorial - SIGIR 2015

Organizers

Manish Gupta - Microsoft

Michael Bendersky - Google, Inc.

Summary

Over the past decade, information retrieval community has deeply explored the problem of transforming natural language verbose queries using operations like reduction, weighting, expansion, reformulation and segmentation into more effective structural representations. However, thus far, there was not a coherent and organized tutorial on this topic. In this tutorial, we aim to put together various research pieces of the puzzle, provide a comprehensive and structured overview of various proposed methods, and also list various application scenarios where effective verbose query processing can make a significant difference.

Our tutorial is suitable for information retrieval researchers and practitioners alike. We will cover both the theory of retrieval methods that deal with verbose queries, as well as provide examples of their practical applications. For the newcomers to the field, the tutorial will give the a complete picture of the current work, introduce important research topics in this field, and hopefully inspire to learn more.

The tutorial will be held as a full-day tutorial at the SIGIR 2015 conference in Santiago, Chile on Sunday, August 9th, 2015. See SIGIR 2015 tutorial page for more details regarding exact time and location.

Tutorial topics

  • Properties of verbose queries.
  • Query reduction
  • Query expansion, reformulation and segmentation.
  • Weighting query concepts.
  • Query dependent learning-to-rank
  • Applications of verbose queries and future research directions.
